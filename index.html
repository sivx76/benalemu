About:

Mobile Applications Developer working in healthcare, leading a nonprofit and designing new websites.

Benyam Alemu currently works as a Mobile Applications Developer for UCSD Health. He builds iOS applications that patients use. He leads a research laboratory known as the Umano Lab for Digital Health (umanolab.org) - a group that makes software for doctors. He has proven design, engineering and operational management skills.

He worked as a Product Manager at Sympto Health and Data Governance Specialist at UCSD Health. He also interned in 7 biomedical research labs across UC San Diego and Harvard Medical School. In May 2014, Ben co-founded SENDforC (sendforc.org), a mentorship nonprofit that teaches elementary to high school students advanced science and engineering topics.

Ben has received notable awards for his leadership initative and innovations. This include being elected to the National Science Foundation Innovation Corps, the UCSD Changemaker Steering Committee, awarded the Westly Prize for the Young Innovators of California (named a top 10 Californian under the age of 28) and the City of Downey Mayor's DNA award - the top honor from his hometown.
